# Source Map

This map links reusable methods to source clusters. It is a navigation aid, not a substitute for reading the relevant theme or figure file.

## How To Use

1. Pick the method the user needs.
2. Load the primary theme.
3. Use the source anchors to ground the concept.
4. State the historical task and boundary before applying it to today.

## Source Anchors

Use text titles as anchors, not as decorative quotations.

| Method | Source anchors | What to look for | Boundary warning |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Historical materialism | `The German Ideology`, `Preface to A Contribution to the Critique of Political Economy`, `Socialism: Utopian and Scientific` | Material life, social relations, ideology, historical stages | Do not reduce all causality to economics |
| Political economy | `Capital` Vol. I, `Wage Labour and Capital`, `Value, Price and Profit`, `Grundrisse` selections | Commodity form, labor, surplus value, accumulation, machinery | Do not turn critique of capital into moral complaint only |
| Class and revolution | `The Communist Manifesto`, `The Class Struggles in France`, `The Eighteenth Brumaire`, `Critique of the Gotha Programme` | Class position, political form, alliance, transition | Do not map nineteenth-century class forms mechanically onto today |
| State | `The Civil War in France`, Engels on the state, Lenin's `The State and Revolution` | State apparatus, coercion, administration, political form | Do not treat every institution as identical in every stage |
| Organization | Lenin's `What Is To Be Done?`, `One Step Forward, Two Steps Back`, revolutionary-party writings | Discipline, consciousness, coordination, leadership | Do not universalize underground or emergency conditions |
| Imperialism | Lenin's `Imperialism, the Highest Stage of Capitalism` | Monopoly, finance capital, uneven development, world division | Do not use "imperialism" as a label without tracing mechanisms |
| Concrete class investigation | Mao's `Analysis of the Classes in Chinese Society`, `Report on an Investigation of the Peasant Movement in Hunan` | Who is friend/enemy, who has which interests and capacity | Do not copy historical class categories without investigation |
| Investigation and practice | Mao's `Oppose Book Worship`, `On Practice` | Direct experience, investigation, knowledge-action cycle | Do not claim practice supports a line before defining feedback |
| Contradiction | Mao's `On Contradiction` | Main contradiction, principal aspect, transformation | Do not call every visible problem the main contradiction |
| Strategy and base areas | Mao's `On Protracted War`, `A Single Spark Can Start a Prairie Fire`, base-area writings | Stage, strategic patience, weak-to-strong transition | Do not romanticize delay when conditions are deteriorating |
| Mass line | Mao's `Some Questions Concerning Methods of Leadership`, investigation prefaces | From masses to masses, synthesis, return, testing | Do not equate popularity with correctness |
| Development and reform | Deng's reform, opening, productivity, and Southern Talks source cluster | Stage judgment, productive forces, practice standard | Do not reduce all judgment to growth numbers |
| Sinicization | Mao on new democracy and Chinese conditions; Deng on socialism with Chinese characteristics | Adapt universal method to concrete conditions | Do not hide contradictions under "localization" |
