# Constraint Map

Use this file before choosing a route for a real-world problem. It prevents jumping from theory to advice before mapping the actual constraints.

## Core Question

```text
What concrete constraint makes this problem hard right now?
```

Do not treat all constraints as equal. Find which constraint currently sets the boundary of possible action.

## Constraint Layers

| Layer | Ask | Evidence |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Material and technical | What physical, technical, time, money, or infrastructure limit exists? | Cost, capacity, tools, supply, time budget, skills |
| Productive forces | What new capability or bottleneck changes what can be produced? | Automation, labor productivity, knowledge, tooling, scale |
| Production relations | Who owns or controls the means, data, platform, capital, workplace, or channel? | Ownership, contracts, wage relation, dependency, platform rules |
| Organization capacity | Can the actors coordinate, decide, execute, and learn? | Team size, decision rights, rituals, feedback loops, legitimacy |
| Information flow | Who sees reality directly, and where is information distorted? | Reports, dashboards, user contact, field investigation, incentives to hide bad news |
| Incentive structure | What behavior is rewarded even if people say the opposite? | Pay, promotion, status, metrics, penalties, risk transfer |
| Ideology and narrative | What story makes the current relation seem natural or inevitable? | Slogans, common sense, identity labels, moral language, taboo questions |

## Projection Rule

When a surface problem repeats, project it downward:

```text
surface symptom -> recurring relation -> constraint layer -> main contradiction candidate
```

Example:

```text
"Team has no execution"
-> decisions change every week
-> information and authority are not coupled
-> contradiction between centralized authority and distributed experience
```

## Common Mistakes

- Do not call a personal failure before mapping material, organizational, and incentive constraints.
- Do not call a technology progressive before asking who controls it and who captures surplus.
- Do not call a policy realistic before asking which class, organization, or stage constraint it answers.
- Do not optimize a process before asking whether the demand should exist.
- Do not solve an information problem with discipline language.
