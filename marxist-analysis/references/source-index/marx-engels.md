# Marx Engels Source Index

Use this file when the answer needs author-only grounding for Marx and Engels. Treat these as source anchors for method extraction, not as quotation stock.

## Inclusion Rule

Include only Marx's or Engels's own works, speeches, prefaces, letters, and jointly authored texts. Exclude biographies, textbooks, readers' guides, and later Marxist interpretations unless the answer explicitly asks for later interpretation.

## Core Text Map

| Text cluster | Author | Use it for | Method granularity | Boundary |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| `Economic and Philosophic Manuscripts of 1844` | Marx | Alienation, labor, species-being, early critique of political economy | Trace how labor becomes external to the worker under private property | Do not use early manuscripts as a finished theory of capital |
| `The Condition of the Working Class in England` | Engels | Industrial capitalism, working-class life, urban social reproduction | Connect production, housing, health, family, and class formation | Do not treat one national case as universal without conditions |
| `The German Ideology` | Marx/Engels | Historical materialism, ideology, social relations | Start from material life-production before ideas and institutions | Do not reduce ideas to passive reflections |
| `The Communist Manifesto` | Marx/Engels | Bourgeois society, class struggle, world market, revolutionary horizon | Identify class positions and the historical movement of capital | Do not use manifesto language as a substitute for concrete class analysis |
| `Wage Labour and Capital`; `Value, Price and Profit` | Marx | Wage relation, labor power, profit, worker bargaining | Distinguish wage appearance from surplus extraction | Do not collapse all labor conflict into wage level only |
| `The Class Struggles in France`; `The Eighteenth Brumaire of Louis Bonaparte` | Marx | Political representation, class fractions, state form, crisis | Map which class forces can act through which political forms | Do not mechanically copy nineteenth-century French class names |
| `Grundrisse` | Marx | Machinery, circulation, crisis tendency, pre-Capital exploration | Use as conceptual laboratory before relying on `Capital` | Mark as drafts when precision matters |
| `Preface to A Contribution to the Critique of Political Economy` | Marx | Productive forces, relations of production, social transformation | Ask whether relations enable or fetter development | Do not turn stages into a rigid ladder |
| `Capital` Vol. I | Marx | Commodity, value, labor process, surplus value, accumulation, machinery | Move from market appearance to production relation | Do not reduce `Capital` to moral criticism of greed |
| `Capital` Vol. II and Vol. III | Marx/Engels edited | Circulation, reproduction, turnover, profit, rent, crisis | Track capital reproduction across production and circulation | Note Engels's editorial role when precision matters |
| `The Civil War in France` | Marx | State form, commune, working-class political power | Ask what administrative and coercive form must be transformed | Do not equate every local institution with the Paris Commune |
| `Anti-Duhring`; `Socialism: Utopian and Scientific` | Engels | Scientific socialism, dialectics, critique of system-building | Separate historical mechanism from utopian blueprint | Do not use simplified summaries to replace Marx's economic argument |
| `Critique of the Gotha Programme` | Marx | Transition, distribution, programmatic limits | Distinguish first-phase limits from higher communist horizon | Do not use transition formulas outside their stated conditions |
| `The Origin of the Family, Private Property and the State` | Engels | Family, property, state, social reproduction | Connect kinship/property forms to production and inheritance | Treat anthropology claims cautiously against later research |
| `Ludwig Feuerbach and the End of Classical German Philosophy` | Engels | Materialism, idealism, German philosophy, practice | Place Marxism after classical German philosophy | Do not turn it into a full substitute for Marx's own method |

## Method Extraction Tests

Use these tests before treating a text as a durable method source:

- Cross-domain reproduction: the same method appears across economy, class, state, ideology, or historical writing.
- Generativity: the method helps analyze a new object without merely repeating the text's conclusion.
- Boundary clarity: the text's historical object and limit are named before applying it to today.
- Non-duplication: multiple passages from one text do not count as source diversity.
