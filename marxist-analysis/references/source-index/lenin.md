# Lenin Source Index

Use this file when the answer needs author-only grounding for Lenin. Treat Lenin's texts as route and organization sources under specific crisis conditions, not as universal instructions for every organization.

## Inclusion Rule

Include only Lenin's own works, reports, theses, speeches, letters, and party documents authored by Lenin. Exclude biographies, histories of Bolshevism, and later Leninist summaries unless explicitly requested.

## Core Text Map

| Text cluster | Use it for | Method granularity | Boundary |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| `What Is To Be Done?` | Spontaneity, consciousness, political leadership, press and organization | Distinguish diffuse grievance from organized political capacity | Do not import underground-party answers into ordinary teams without translating conditions |
| `One Step Forward, Two Steps Back` | Party rules, organization discipline, faction conflict | Ask what organizational form can actually coordinate action | Do not make discipline a substitute for correct line or legitimacy |
| `Two Tactics of Social-Democracy in the Democratic Revolution` | Democratic revolution, alliance, tactical route | Match tactics to stage and class alliance | Do not treat one revolutionary stage as permanent |
| `Materialism and Empirio-Criticism` | Philosophical materialism, anti-idealism | Defend external reality and practice against subjectivism | Do not use it as a shortcut around concrete investigation |
| `Imperialism, the Highest Stage of Capitalism` | Monopoly, finance capital, export of capital, uneven world system | Link domestic contradictions to world hierarchy and monopoly structure | Do not use imperialism as a label without tracing mechanisms |
| `The State and Revolution` | State machine, coercion, bureaucracy, transition | Ask who controls coercion and administration, and whether the apparatus is transformed | Do not treat every institution in every stage as identical |
| `The Tasks of the Proletariat in the Present Revolution` / April Theses | Route change under fast-moving crisis | Reassess stage when facts change; reject yesterday's correct line if conditions move | Do not treat tactical rupture as permanent impatience |
| `The Impending Catastrophe and How to Combat It` | Crisis management, state control, banking, production administration | Convert crisis diagnosis into concrete control levers | Do not confuse emergency measures with normal policy |
| `Left-Wing Communism: An Infantile Disorder` | Tactical flexibility, parliament, compromise, mass connection | Separate principled goal from tactical form | Do not use flexibility to justify opportunism without stage analysis |
| `The Tax in Kind`; `Fourth Anniversary of the October Revolution`; `The New Economic Policy and the Tasks of the Political Education Departments` | Retreat, market concessions, peasant alliance, recovery | Treat retreat as a staged policy under material constraint | Do not call every market measure NEP without naming constraint and control |
| `Better Fewer, But Better` | Bureaucracy, state capacity, inspection, quality over quantity | Improve apparatus by reducing noise and raising quality | Do not mistake smaller scale for solved power relations |

## Method Extraction Tests

- Apparatus test: name the coercive, administrative, financial, or organizational apparatus that decides outcomes.
- Organization test: separate discontent, consciousness, coordination, discipline, and legitimacy.
- Stage test: identify whether the text answers democratic revolution, imperialist war, insurrection, civil war, NEP, or state consolidation.
- Mechanism test: for imperialism, trace monopoly, finance, capital export, unevenness, or world division before using the term.

| Late state apparatus criticism | `Better Fewer, But Better` | Why a victorious organization can still fail through bureaucracy and low state capacity |
