# Mao

## Historical Task

Apply Marxism to Chinese revolutionary and construction conditions through contradiction analysis, investigation, practice testing, mass line, united front, and strategic patience.

## Strongest Use

- Main contradiction and contradiction transformation.
- Practice and investigation.
- Mass line and leadership method.
- Base area, united front, protracted strategy.
- Personal and organizational practice loops.

## Method Moves

1. Ask "where is the contradiction?"
2. Find the main contradiction and its main aspect.
3. Investigate concrete conditions before speaking.
4. Convert analysis into a small base area or test.
5. Return to practice and revise.

## Method Signatures

- Starts by locating the decisive contradiction, not by listing all problems.
- Treats investigation as a condition for speech.
- Converts strategy into stages and base areas.
- Distinguishes enemy/friend, antagonistic/non-antagonistic, principal/secondary.
- Returns theory to practice through a small test.

## Diagnostic Questions

- What is the main contradiction right now?
- What is only a symptom or secondary contradiction?
- What direct investigation has been done?
- Where is the small base area that can produce evidence?
- Which forces can be united, neutralized, or opposed?

## Boundaries

- Do not turn every problem into struggle language.
- Do not skip investigation because the framework feels powerful.
- Do not confuse optimism about transformation with guaranteed success.
- Do not call something "main contradiction" unless solving it changes the rest of the situation.
