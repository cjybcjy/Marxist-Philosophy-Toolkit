# Lenin

## Historical Task

Analyze imperialism, state power, party organization, revolutionary strategy, and the problem of coordinated political action under crisis conditions.

## Strongest Use

- State machine and coercive apparatus.
- Party organization and discipline.
- Imperialism and monopoly capital.
- Strategic route under hostile institutional conditions.

## Method Moves

1. Ask which apparatus actually holds power.
2. Distinguish spontaneous discontent from organized force.
3. Analyze international and monopoly-capital conditions.
4. Ask what organization form can act under pressure.

## Method Signatures

- Refuses to treat state power as neutral procedure.
- Converts diffuse dissatisfaction into the question of organization.
- Treats strategy as stage-specific, not as permanent formula.
- Connects domestic politics to international capitalist hierarchy.

## Diagnostic Questions

- What apparatus enforces the current order?
- What is spontaneous and what is organized?
- Who has the capacity to coordinate beyond local anger?
- Is the conflict domestic only, or tied to monopoly/global pressure?
- What route is possible under the current balance of forces?

## Boundaries

- Do not universalize revolutionary emergency conditions.
- Do not use organization discipline as a substitute for correct analysis.
- Do not ignore mass legitimacy and practical conditions.
- Do not import party-form answers into ordinary team problems without translating the conditions.
