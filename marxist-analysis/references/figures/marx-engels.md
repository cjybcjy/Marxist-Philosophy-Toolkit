# Marx Engels

## Historical Task

Analyze capitalist society, historical development, class relations, commodity production, exploitation, and the material basis of social change.

## Strongest Use

- Capital and commodity analysis.
- Labor, surplus, exploitation, accumulation.
- Historical materialism and class formation.
- Critique of ideology as rooted in social relations.

## Method Moves

1. Start from material production and social reproduction.
2. Analyze relations, not isolated individuals.
3. Look for the hidden social relation behind an apparently neutral market form.
4. Ask how contradictions of capital reproduce crisis.

## Method Signatures

- Converts moral or legal appearance into social relation analysis.
- Looks for the production relation behind the commodity, wage, contract, or institution.
- Explains ideas through lived social relations without denying that ideas act back on practice.
- Treats class as a structural position, not a lifestyle label.

## Diagnostic Questions

- What is being produced, and under what labor relation?
- Who owns or controls the means, platform, data, infrastructure, or capital?
- Where is surplus captured?
- What contradiction does accumulation intensify?
- Which apparent freedom hides dependence?

## Boundaries

- Do not use this module alone for later party organization or development-stage policy.
- Do not turn Marx into moral complaint about unfairness only.
- Do not treat every modern policy problem as directly answered by nineteenth-century texts.
- Do not quote "class struggle" without mapping concrete class positions.
