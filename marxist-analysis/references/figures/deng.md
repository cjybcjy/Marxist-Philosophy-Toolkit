# Deng

## Historical Task

Address development stage, productivity, reform, opening, institutional adjustment, and practice as the standard for testing policy routes.

## Source Anchors

| Source cluster | Use it for |
| --- | --- |
| Emancipating the mind and seeking truth from facts | Breaking doctrinal blockage through practice |
| Socialist modernization and primary-stage discussions | Development-stage judgment |
| Reform and opening speeches | Institutional adjustment, incentives, external learning |
| Productivity-standard discussions | Binding constraint and practical result |
| Southern Talks source cluster | Route confidence, market mechanism, development urgency |

## Strongest Use

- Development-stage judgment.
- Productivity as a binding constraint.
- Reform and opening as practical route.
- Policy tradeoffs under imperfect conditions.
- "What works under current conditions?" questions.

## Method Moves

1. Ask what stage the society or organization is actually in.
2. Identify the binding constraint on development.
3. Prefer practice results over doctrinal appearance.
4. Treat reform as an experiment with risks and feedback.
5. Ask what new contradiction the solution creates.

## Method Signatures

- Judges a route by whether it releases capacity under current conditions.
- Treats stage as a constraint on what can be built immediately.
- Uses practice results to break formulaic debate.
- Accepts experimentation, zones, pilots, and feedback.
- Watches for new contradictions after reform succeeds.

## Diagnostic Questions

- What is the current stage, not the desired final form?
- What is the binding constraint on development or capacity?
- Which existing relation blocks productive forces or social reproduction?
- What pilot can test the route?
- Who gains, who loses, and what new contradiction appears?

## Boundaries

- Do not reduce Marxism to growth alone.
- Do not use "practice standard" to avoid value or class analysis.
- Do not assume market mechanisms are automatically correct.
- Do not call a short-term result proof without checking distribution, dependency, and long-term capacity.
