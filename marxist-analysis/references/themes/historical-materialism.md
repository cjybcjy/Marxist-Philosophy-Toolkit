# Historical Materialism

## Use For

Use this theme when the problem concerns social development, material conditions, technology, production relations, institutions, or why ideas alone cannot explain change.

## Core Lens

Start from how people reproduce life:

```text
productive forces -> production relations -> social structure -> political and ideological forms
```

This is not economic determinism. Ideas, politics, and organization matter, but they work inside material conditions and social relations.

## Source Anchors

- Marx/Engels on material production and ideology.
- Marx's preface on productive forces and production relations.
- Engels on scientific socialism and historical development.

Use these as method anchors: explain how social relations arise from material life, not as proof that economics mechanically determines every event.

## Analysis Moves

1. Identify the material base: technology, labor process, resources, infrastructure.
2. Identify production relations: ownership, control, coordination, distribution.
3. Ask what relation now blocks or releases productive capacity.
4. Ask which groups benefit from the current arrangement.
5. Ask what new relation or institution is becoming possible.

## Common Misuse

- Reducing everything to money.
- Treating technology as automatically progressive.
- Ignoring organization and political struggle.
- Explaining history as moral awakening alone.

## Boundary Test

If the answer says "because ideas changed" but never asks who works, who owns, who coordinates, and who benefits, it is not using historical materialism.

## Output Prompt

```text
这个问题的物质条件是什么？
谁控制关键生产资料或组织资源？
现有关系促进了什么，又阻碍了什么？
```
