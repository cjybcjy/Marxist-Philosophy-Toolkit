# Political Economy

## Use For

Use this theme for capital, commodity, labor, value, profit, exploitation, platform economics, industry structure, financialization, and company analysis.

## Core Lens

Do not start with whether actors are good or bad. Start with the circuit of value:

```text
inputs -> labor process -> commodity/service -> market realization -> surplus capture -> reinvestment/control
```

## Source Anchors

- Marx's critique of political economy for commodity, value, wage labor, surplus value, machinery, accumulation, and crisis.
- Marx/Engels on class and property relations for the social form behind market exchange.

Use these anchors to analyze value relations and power, not merely to condemn profit.

## Analysis Moves

1. Identify the commodity or service being sold.
2. Identify whose labor creates value and who captures surplus.
3. Identify ownership and control of key assets.
4. Identify market dependency, pricing power, and bargaining power.
5. Identify whether productivity gains free people or intensify extraction.
6. Identify crisis points: overcapacity, demand limits, debt, labor conflict, regulation.

## Company/Industry Questions

- What is the unit of value?
- Who owns the bottleneck?
- Who bears risk?
- Who is replaceable and who is not?
- Does scale reduce human labor or merely hide it?
- What contradiction would worsen if the industry grows?

## Common Misuse

- Treating revenue growth as social progress.
- Treating all technology as emancipation.
- Ignoring unpaid, informal, or hidden labor.
- Confusing consumer benefit with worker or social benefit.

## Boundary Test

If the analysis cannot say who creates value, who captures surplus, who owns the bottleneck, and who bears risk, it is not yet political economy.
