# Reform Development

## Use For

Use this theme for development stage, productivity, reform, opening, policy tradeoffs, modernization, and "why not apply the ideal immediately" questions.

## Core Lens

Ask what stage the system is in and what relation currently blocks development. Reform is not automatically betrayal or correctness. It must be judged by conditions, results, class effects, and whether it expands future capacity.

## Source Anchors

- Deng's source cluster on emancipating the mind, seeking truth from facts, productivity, reform, opening, and primary-stage judgment.
- Marxist historical materialism for the relation between productive forces and production relations.
- Mao's practice method for testing policy through concrete results.

Use these anchors to judge staged reform. Do not use them as a blank check for any policy called "reform."

## Analysis Moves

1. Identify the development stage and binding constraint.
2. Ask whether existing relations block productive forces or social reproduction.
3. Identify the reform target: ownership, incentives, administration, technology, market access, or distribution.
4. Identify who benefits, who pays, and what new contradiction appears.
5. Define the practice standard: what result would prove the route works?

## Common Misuse

- Reducing all questions to GDP or efficiency.
- Calling every market mechanism reform.
- Calling every compromise betrayal.
- Ignoring the contradictions created by successful reform.

## Boundary Test

If a reform answer does not say what new contradiction the reform creates, it is too smooth to be trusted.

## Practice Standard

Use practice results, not purity of wording, to test a route. But also test distribution and long-term capacity, not only short-term growth.
