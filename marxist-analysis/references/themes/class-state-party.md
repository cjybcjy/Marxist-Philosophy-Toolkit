# Class State Party

## Use For

Use this theme for class position, state power, party organization, organizational discipline, institutional conflict, and political strategy.

## Core Lens

Class is not just income identity. It is position in production, ownership, control, and dependence. The state is not a neutral committee; it organizes coercion, law, legitimacy, and administration within a concrete social order.

## Source Anchors

- Marx/Engels for class position, political form, and the state as a social relation.
- Lenin for state machine, party organization, imperialism, and coordinated political force.
- Mao for concrete class investigation, united front, and handling different kinds of contradictions.

Use these anchors to map power and organization. Do not use them to slap labels on people.

## Analysis Moves

1. Identify class or organizational positions, not just opinions.
2. Identify who controls coercion, rules, budgets, appointments, or infrastructure.
3. Identify the organization form: loose network, bureaucracy, party, firm, movement.
4. Identify coordination problem: discipline, legitimacy, representation, or capacity.
5. Identify whether conflict is antagonistic, non-antagonistic, or mixed.

## Organization Questions

- Who can decide?
- Who can block?
- Who bears consequences?
- Who speaks for whom?
- What mechanism turns scattered dissatisfaction into organized force?

## Common Misuse

- Reducing class to lifestyle.
- Treating state institutions as merely personal choices.
- Demanding discipline when the real problem is legitimacy.
- Demanding participation when the real problem is strategic coordination.

## Boundary Test

If the answer cannot identify who decides, who executes, who blocks, and who bears consequences, it has not yet analyzed organization.
